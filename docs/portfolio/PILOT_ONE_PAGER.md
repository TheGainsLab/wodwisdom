## Side 1 — The class you can add tomorrow

**You know the problem with adding a class: everyone has to be there at the
same time.** Your members don't have an extra hour at 9am Saturday. So the
class never happens, and the rowers sit idle at 2pm.

**Engine is a conditioning class your members do whenever they want.**
A real program — progressive, year-round, written by an engine that's been
training competitive athletes — not a random WOD generator. Members see it on
their phone, do it when they can, log their numbers, and land on **your gym's
leaderboard**.

- **Members:** the workout on their phone, their numbers, their trends, a
  built-in coach that answers questions about today's session.
- **You:** a new class on the schedule with zero coaching hours, zero floor
  time, and a leaderboard that keeps people coming back. Fair rankings, too —
  our scoring is physics-based (watts per kilo), so your 130-lb member
  competes honestly with your 220-lb member. Divisions by gender and modality.
- **The math:** you pay **$6/month per member who's in the class.** Nothing to
  set up, no platform fee. You charge whatever you want — most gyms will do
  $15–25/month or fold it into a premium tier. Twenty members at $20 is
  **$400/month of new revenue against $120 in cost,** for a class nobody has
  to coach at 6am.

Every other idea the industry gives you — more PT sessions, nutrition
coaching, semi-privates — costs *your hours*. This one doesn't.

## Side 2 — Your programming, back in your hands

**Be honest about what outsourced programming did to your gym.** You pay
$100–300/month for a famous template, and in exchange you lost the thing your
members actually pay YOU for: knowing how they're doing. You know about their
kids' softball games. You don't know that Steve's pulling strength stalled
three weeks ago and he's about to quietly cancel.

**It's not your fault — you've had no instrumentation. That's what we built.**

1. **Take control back.** Tell the AI how you want your gym to run — your
   style, your month's focus, your equipment. It writes YOUR programming, in
   your voice. You approve it or say "less overhead volume in week 3" and it
   rewrites. Ten minutes a month.
2. **Every member gets their version.** Same workout on the floor — same
   blocks, same class, same community — but each member's phone shows *their*
   loads, computed from *their* actual numbers, with injuries and limitations
   handled. No more guessing at percentages on the whiteboard.
3. **And for the first time, your programming reports back.** PRs as they
   happen. Who's stalling. Who's gone quiet — *before* they cancel, not after.
   With a suggested conversation, delivered by you, the human they joined for.

> **Your leaderboard tells you who won today.
> We tell you who's quietly losing — three weeks before they cancel.**

- **The math:** **$149/month + $3 per member** — inside what you already pay
  for a template that personalizes no one and reports nothing. One saved
  $150 membership per month pays for a 50-member gym by itself.
- **No leap of faith:** we run in **shadow mode** first — the AI writes your
  programming privately, next to what you're paying for now. Compare for a
  month. Switch when it wins.
