## The pitch (to the gym owner)

**Give your members the proven Engine program — the same one we sell to individuals for
$30 — at a price you set.** They install it on their phone, pick a track, and start on
Day 1; they get the full experience (breakdowns, history, the embedded day coach). You
buy at $6/seat and resell for whatever you want. Zero programming work, zero content
cost — pure distribution margin.

- **$0 setup.** No install fee, no equipment purchase.
- **$6 / active seat / month** wholesale (what you pay us). Volume breaks: **$5 at
  100+ seats, $4 at 200+.**
- **10-seat minimum** from launch (a 60-day grace period bills your actual seats
  first). **Founding partners: 50% off** for the founding term.
- You are billed **only for active seats** — a member who stops logging for 60 days
  auto-deactivates (we nudge them at 45), so you never pay for ghosts.

## What you charge your members (you decide — we never touch your member pricing)

Two proven models:

| Model | How it works | Feels like |
|---|---|---|
| **Add-on** | $15–$25 / member / month on top of membership | A premium class package |
| **Baked in** | Fold into a dues tier (e.g. the "+ Engine" tier is $20 more) | A membership upgrade |

## The margin math (add-on model)

Your cost is $6/seat (less at scale, less as a founding partner). At a $20 retail add-on:

| Active seats | Your cost (@ $6) | Your revenue (@ $20) | **Your margin / mo** | Margin / yr |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 10 (minimum) | $60 | $200 | **$140** | $1,680 |
| 25 | $150 | $500 | **$350** | $4,200 |
| 50 | $300 | $1,000 | **$700** | $8,400 |
| 100 (@ $5 cost) | $500 | $2,000 | **$1,500** | $18,000 |
| 200 (@ $4 cost) | $800 | $4,000 | **$3,200** | $38,400 |

**Founding partner, first term (50% off cost):** a 50-seat gym's cost drops from $300
to **$150** → margin **$850/mo** at a $20 retail. The seat cost only goes down as you
grow (100+ → $5, 200+ → $4); your retail price doesn't have to.

> Rule of thumb: **every active seat is $9–$19/month of gross margin** ($15–$25 retail
> minus your $6 cost), before your scale discounts widen it further. 20 members covers
> the class; everyone past that is contribution to rent.
