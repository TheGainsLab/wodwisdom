# Founding Partner Agreement — Outline (for legal review)

> NOT a contract — the structured outline a lawyer turns into one. Every term
> here traces to a decided item in STRATEGY.md / GYM_SKU_SPEC.md /
> BILLING_MECHANICS_SPEC.md. Keep the final document SHORT (target: 2 pages) —
> the counterparty is a gym owner, not a procurement department, and the deal
> is a pilot, not an enterprise MSA.

## 1. Parties & term

- The Gains Lab (legal entity TBD) and the gym (entity + owner signatory).
- **Term: 6 months** from first member activation (not signature date — the
  clock starts when the pilot is real). Auto-converts to standard monthly
  terms at expiry unless either side exits (§8).

## 2. What the partner receives

- **50% off all platform fees** for the term (Engine Class seats, Analytics,
  and — if activated during the term — AI Gym Programmer base + roster).
- **Minimums waived** (the 10-seat Engine Class minimum does not apply).
- Founding Partner status: direct line to the founder; input on roadmap;
  named early access to new capabilities.
- 30-day written notice before standard pricing begins at term end
  (BILLING_MECHANICS_SPEC §10).

## 3. What the partner commits to

- **Honest feedback**: a short structured check-in (call or written) monthly.
- **Case study**: cooperate in producing one written case study; gym named or
  anonymous at the gym's choice; gym approves quotes/metrics before
  publication.
- **Reference**: up to two reference calls with prospective gyms, once the
  partner is satisfied (not before — don't force advocacy).
- **A real launch**: promote the Engine Class to members (poster/announcement
  minimum). A pilot with zero activated members teaches nothing; the partner
  agrees the intent is live usage.

## 4. Data terms (the clean-provenance foundation — lawyer attention here)

- **Gym-level consent** (signed here): aggregated, de-identified gym
  performance data may be used to improve The Gains Lab's services and in
  benchmarking products; the gym's programming and methodology remain the
  gym's (see §6).
- **Member-level consent** (collected in-product at member signup, not in
  this agreement): each member consents to their performance data being
  processed to deliver the service (scaling, leaderboards, trends) and used
  in de-identified aggregate. The agreement should reference that this
  consent flow exists and the gym won't circumvent it (e.g., no bulk account
  creation on members' behalf without their consent).
- **Portability commitment (both directions)**: member data belongs to the
  member's account; if a member leaves the gym or the gym leaves the
  platform, members may retain their accounts/history (convert to retail).
  The gym receives its aggregate reports; it does not own members' individual
  training histories on our platform.
- No sale of member personal data to third parties. Period.

## 5. Pricing mechanics referenced (not restated)

- Per-seat wholesale; gym prices its members freely (we provide suggested
  retail guidance only) — with ONE exception:
- **Remote-membership floor**: if the gym sells remote memberships, the
  member-facing price must be ≥ The Gains Lab's current retail bundle price
  (contractual enforcement per BILLING_MECHANICS_SPEC §8).
- Billing: monthly via Stripe; active-seat snapshot billing; the portal
  always shows the counts behind the invoice.

## 6. Intellectual property

- **The gym's programming, methodology, and brand remain the gym's.** Any
  methodology corpus uploaded (Programmer, if activated) is licensed to us
  solely to operate the service for that gym, revocable on exit.
- **The platform (Engine, models, software, aggregate insights) remains
  ours.** No exclusivity in either direction.
- Case study content is jointly approved (§3).

## 7. Service posture (keep modest — it's a pilot)

- Commercially reasonable uptime; no formal SLA during the pilot.
- Support: direct founder channel, business-hours response intent.
- **Fitness disclaimer**: programming is training guidance, not medical
  advice; members should have appropriate health clearance; the gym remains
  responsible for coaching supervision and member safety on its floor.
  (Lawyer: standard fitness-industry liability language + our audit layer
  described accurately — we verify programs against stated athlete data; we
  do not supervise training.)

## 8. Exit

- Either party may exit with **30 days' notice**, effective end of billing
  period. No termination fees.
- On exit: gym-sourced member entitlements expire at period end; members are
  offered retail conversion (portability, §4); gym data export of its own
  aggregate reports; methodology corpus deleted on request.
- Our exit right matters too (a bad-fit pilot should end cleanly).

## 9. Boring but necessary

- Confidentiality (mutual, lightweight — pricing terms + non-public product
  info).
- No assignment without consent; governing law (founder's choice);
  entire-agreement clause.
